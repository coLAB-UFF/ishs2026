  <h2 class="mb-4">Culture, History, and Iconic Landmarks</h2>
  <p><strong>Christ the Redeemer</strong></p>
  <p>One of the New Seven Wonders of the World, this iconic statue atop Corcovado Mountain offers breathtaking panoramic views of Rio de Janeiro.</p>
  <br>
  <p><strong>Sugarloaf Mountain (Pão de Açúcar)</strong></p>
  <p>Take the cable car to the summit for sweeping views of the city, Guanabara Bay, and the surrounding mountains.</p>
  <br>
  <p><strong>Selarón Steps (Escadaria Selarón)</strong></p>
  <p>A colorful mosaic staircase created by artist Jorge Selarón, connecting the neighborhoods of Lapa and Santa Teresa and offering a vibrant photo opportunity.</p>
  <br>
  <p><strong>Royal Portuguese Reading Room (Real Gabinete Português de Leitura)</strong></p>
  <p>A historic library with stunning 19th-century architecture and a vast collection of Portuguese literature.</p>
  <br>
  <p><strong>Theatro Municipal</strong></p>
  <p>A beautiful historic theater in downtown Rio, hosting concerts, opera, and cultural events.</p>
  <br>
  <br>
  <h2 class="mb-4">Nature and Outdoor Recreation</h2>
  <p><strong>Copacabana Beach</strong></p>
  <p>One of the world’s most famous beaches, ideal for sunbathing, walking along the promenade, and enjoying beachfront kiosks and restaurants.</p>
  <br>
  <p><strong>Ipanema Beach</strong></p>
  <p>Known for its vibrant atmosphere and stunning views, Ipanema is perfect for swimming, volleyball, or people-watching at sunset.</p>
  <br>
  <p><strong>Tijuca National Park</strong></p>
  <p>A massive urban rainforest offering hiking trails, waterfalls, and wildlife, including trails to Pico da Tijuca and the famous Pedra Bonita viewpoint.</p>
  <br>
  <p><strong>Botanical Garden (Jardim Botânico)</strong></p>
  <p>A serene park featuring tropical plants, towering palm trees, and peaceful walking paths, ideal for nature lovers.</p>
  <br>
  <p><strong>Lagoa Rodrigo de Freitas</strong></p>
  <p>A scenic lagoon surrounded by a walking and cycling path, perfect for jogging, paddleboarding, or enjoying outdoor cafes.</p>
  <br>
  <p><strong>Maracanã Stadium</strong></p>
  <p>An iconic sports venue offering guided tours and occasional football matches, a must-visit for sports enthusiasts.</p>
  <br>
---

There are numerous attractions and tours in Rio de Janeiro. Visitors can stroll along famous beaches like Copacabana and Ipanema, enjoy scenic hikes in Tijuca National Park, or explore the city’s vibrant cultural landmarks such as Christ the Redeemer and the Selarón Steps. Boat tours of Guanabara Bay and visits to the Botanical Garden provide relaxing ways to experience Rio’s natural beauty. Combined with its lively neighborhoods, cafes, and local markets, Rio offers an unforgettable blend of culture, nature, and leisure.
